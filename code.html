Coding + Games

I began to learn web and game development during the beginning of the 2020 SARS-CoV-2 pandemic. As an immunocompromised person, I feared for my capacity to work from home with a professional background in the Social Sciences.

Entirely self-taught (with the help of Stack Overflow) I dove into independent game development and the "Handmade Web."

The Handmade Web is a term coined by artist and researcher J.R. Carpenter for a presentation titled Slow Media at Bath Spa University in the United Kingdom. Carpenter writes:

I evoke the term 'handmade web' to refer to web pages coded by hand rather than by software; web pages made and maintained by individuals rather than by businesses or corporations [...] I evoke the term 'handmade web' to suggest slowness and smallness as a forms of resistance. (2015)

Welcome Home, Maya (Summer 2025)

An upcoming point-and-click adventure game using the Adventure Game Studio engine (first released in 1997). A semi-biographical retelling of the author's personal experiences.

The game is currently in production, and will feature responsive UI elements including a smartphone with contacts that update throughout the story, visually-implemented status effects, and hand-drawn pixel art.

A small horror/thriller "ARG" (Alternate Reality Game) using the browser as a game engine, made up of vanilla JS, CSS, and HTML, inspired by the works of director David Cronenberg.

The experience takes about 15 minutes and uses logic puzzles and investigative gameplay as tools to drive the narrative. Inspired by my love for early Internet and text-based escape games of my childhood. All assets, graphics, code, and music are produced by Chaya Friedlander-Abramovitch.

I Grow Still. (2023)

A short game made in a weekend using the tiny Bitsy Color engine by AurySystem, with integration of Borksy Hacks by AYolland.

The game features a short linear narrative on the topic of the SARS-CoV-2 pandemic and the ease with which people moved on from precautions. All pixel art, writing, music, and programming is done by Chaya Friedlander-Abramovitch.
