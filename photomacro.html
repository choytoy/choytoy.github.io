Macro

Les Insectes (2024)

I am captivated by the wonder of the macroscopic world, and its colourful alien inhabitants. Macro photography is a portal through which I can observe the natural world with awe, as it calls attention to the tiniest details.

At a sensor magnification of 1.0x-2.0x, depth of field is so thin that even one's heartbeat can set off an image's focal point. Macro photography, in this sense, is a form of meditation for me -- A way to focus simultaneously on my pulse and breathing while leaning into these natural movements of the body at rest. All bodily sensations are anchored in my perception of the natural world through the lens of my camera.

My photography setup is comprised of a cropped sensor (APS-C) mirrorless SLR camera, paired with a specialty 2x magnification manual focus macro lens. All subjects are photogradphed live in the wild. Images are shot in RAW format to maximize sensor information, then minimally adjusted using Adobe Lightroom.

Click on the sprites to learn more about each insect I've photographed and view images.
Equipment used: Canon EOS R50 Mirrorless Camera, Laowa 65mm 2x Macro Lens, Godox v350 Flash.
